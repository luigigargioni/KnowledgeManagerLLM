# Valium

Valium - Capsules, Oral Drops is a drug based on the active ingredient Diazepam, belonging to the category of Benzodiazepines. Valium is used for the treatment of anxiety, tension, anxiety-related somatic or psychiatric symptoms, and insomnia.

## How to use Valium

Valium should be used for the shortest time possible based on clinical needs. The dose should be individualized by a healthcare professional, especially in elderly or debilitated patients. Treatment should not be stopped abruptly and the dosage should be gradually reduced to avoid withdrawal symptoms.

- Adults: 2 mg two to three times daily up to 5 mg one to two times daily.
- Elderly or debilitated patients: 2 mg twice daily.
- Hospital treatment of severe anxiety states: 10–20 mg three times daily.
- Children: Up to 3 years: 1–6 mg daily; 4–14 years: 4–12 mg daily.
- For insomnia, the medication should be taken immediately before bedtime.
- Avoid alcohol while taking Valium.
- The maximum treatment duration is generally 4 weeks for insomnia and 8–12 weeks for anxiety, including gradual discontinuation.

## When Valium should not be used
- Hypersensitivity to diazepam, other benzodiazepines, or any component of the formulation.
- Myasthenia gravis.
- Severe respiratory insufficiency.
- Severe acute or chronic hepatic insufficiency.
- Sleep apnea syndrome.
- First trimester of pregnancy.
- Breastfeeding.
- Concomitant use with alcohol or other central nervous system depressants without medical supervision.
- Patients with a history of substance or alcohol abuse should use Valium only with extreme caution and under strict medical supervision.


## Contraindications and Warnings
- Valium may cause drowsiness, sedation, reduced alertness, muscle weakness, impaired coordination, and slowed reaction times.
- The medication may impair the ability to perform physical activities, drive vehicles, operate machinery, or engage in tasks requiring concentration and coordination.
- The risk of impaired performance is increased when combined with alcohol or other sedative medications.
- Patients should avoid potentially hazardous activities until they know how Valium affects them.
- Elderly patients may be at increased risk of falls and fractures due to sedation and impaired balance.
- Long-term use may lead to physical and psychological dependence.
